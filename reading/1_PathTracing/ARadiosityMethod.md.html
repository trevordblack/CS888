**A Radiosity Method For Non-Diffuse Environments** # Abstract - A general radiosity method is introduced - It solves for reflections of light among diffuse, non-diffuse, and emissive surfaces # Introduction - RT was the first method introduced to include shadowing and reflections from neighboring surfaces - Given that a limited numbers of rays are traced from the eye - Perfect modeling of GI is impossible - "Only radiosity methods can accurately model GI" - The radiosity method is based on the Law of Conservation of Energy - Is a complex global solution to solve for the intensity of light leaving all surfaces - Solves a set of linear eqs describing transfer of diffuse light between surfaces - Can be completed independently of wavelength - Advantages - Increased accuracy of GI - Viewing independent - Problem: - Previously all radiosity methods assumed all surfaces to be perfect Lambertian - Specular surfaces were not allowed - This paper removes the restriction on surfaces by - generalizing the radiosity formulation
